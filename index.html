HOW AN AWARD-WINNING CHEMISTRY EDUCATION GRADUATE BECOMES A TECH CEO IN NIGERIA

Segun Solomon graduated from his alma mater with a CGPA of 4.72 out of 5 after studying Chemistry Education and minoring in Biology. He was awarded two (2) meritorious academic awards during his convocation from the college in 2017. However, he made the decision to switch from being a school teacher to becoming a tech CEO at age 23, after being a tech enthusiast for the early parts of his post-college days.

So far, Solomon has consulted for two universities, two polytechnics, and a college in Nigeria - each instance on technology, not mentioning diverse high school managements that he has consulted for. He has also consulted for more than twenty-six (26) businesses within the space of two years. His technology academy (Madros Technologies Academy) has been able to train more than one hundred and sixty-four (164) students through direct tutelage for professional technology skills acquisition. Solomon has facilitated more than six (6) sessions with the Nigerian Bottling Company (NBC) , West Africa Vocational Education (WAVE Academy), and SEAD Africa to train more than 2000 young tech enthusiasts in soft and tech skills acquisition in Osun and Ekiti States, Nigeria.

Today, Solomon is a young, award-winning tech consultant, blogger, digital trainer, enVogue tech researcher, on-air tech guide, and content developer. Solomon is competently skilled in front-end web development, data sciences, content writing, cloud computing, graphic design and branding, and digital marketing. Solomon is also technically versed in a variety of computer appreciation skills.
